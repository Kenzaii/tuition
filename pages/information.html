Information & FAQs

Everything you need to know about studying in Singapore as an international student

About Singapore's Education System

Singapore consistently ranks as one of the world's top education systems

Singapore's education system is known for its high standards and rigorous academics. It has consistently ranked among the top in international assessments such as PISA (Programme for International Student Assessment) and TIMSS (Trends in International Mathematics and Science Study).

The system emphasizes strong foundations in mathematics, science, and languages, with a focus on critical thinking and problem-solving skills. Students are encouraged to excel academically while also developing character and values.

For international students, adapting to Singapore's education system can be challenging due to its pace and high expectations. That's where our specialized tuition services can help bridge any gaps and ensure a smooth transition.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about studying in Singapore as an international student

What are the different types of schools in Singapore?

Singapore has several types of schools catering to different needs:

  • Local Public Schools: Government-funded schools following the national curriculum
  • International Schools: Schools offering various international curricula (IB, IGCSE, American, etc.)
  • Private Schools: Independent schools offering a mix of local and international curricula
  • Specialized Schools: Schools focusing on specific areas like sports, arts, or STEM

The appropriate choice depends on your long-term plans, budget, and your child's educational background.

What student visas do international students need to study in Singapore?

International students require a Student's Pass to study in Singapore. The application process typically includes:

  • Securing acceptance from a Singapore educational institution
  • The school will issue a letter of acceptance and initiate the Student's Pass application
  • Students need to complete the e-Form 16 through the Immigration & Checkpoints Authority (ICA) system
  • Upon approval, students will receive an In-Principle Approval (IPA) letter
  • Students must complete the formalities at ICA after arriving in Singapore

The process typically takes 2-4 weeks. We recommend applying at least 2 months before your course starts.

How does the Singapore school year and grading system work?

Academic Year: The school year in Singapore typically runs from January to November/December, with breaks in March, June, and September.

Grading System: Singapore uses different grading systems depending on the level:

  • Primary Schools: Students are assessed on an achievement band (1-4) or grades (A-E)
  • Secondary Schools: Grades typically range from A1 (best) to F9 (fail)
  • Junior Colleges: H1, H2, and H3 subjects are graded from A to E and S (sub-pass)
  • International Schools: Follow their respective curriculum's grading system

The Singapore system places a strong emphasis on examinations, with major national exams at the end of primary school (PSLE), secondary school (O-levels), and junior college (A-levels).

What accommodation options are available for international students?

International students in Singapore have several accommodation options:

  • School Dormitories: Many international schools and some local schools offer boarding facilities
  • Private Hostels: Purpose-built student accommodations with amenities and community activities
  • Homestays: Living with a local family, which provides cultural immersion and supervision
  • Private Rentals: Apartments or condominiums, often shared with other students
  • HDB Flats: Public housing options, which are more affordable but have certain rental restrictions

Costs vary widely depending on the option chosen, location, and facilities. Budget approximately SGD 600-1,500 per month for accommodation. We can provide recommendations based on your specific needs and budget.

What are the living costs for students in Singapore?

Singapore is known to have a relatively high cost of living, but it varies depending on lifestyle. Here's a monthly estimate for students:

  • Accommodation: SGD 600-1,500 (depending on type and location)
  • Food: SGD 300-500 (hawker centers are affordable options at $3-5 per meal)
  • Transportation: SGD 100-150 (using student concession passes)
  • Utilities & Internet: SGD 80-150 (if not included in rent)
  • Books & Supplies: SGD 50-100
  • Entertainment & Misc: SGD 200-400

Total: Approximately SGD 1,330-2,800 per month, not including tuition fees.

Students should also budget for initial costs like security deposits, insurance, and settling-in expenses.

How can international students adapt to Singapore's education system?

Adapting to Singapore's education system can be challenging due to its rigorous nature. Here are some strategies:

  • Start Early: Begin preparation before arriving if possible, especially in mathematics and sciences
  • Seek Specialized Tuition: Work with tutors familiar with both Singapore's system and your educational background (that's where we can help!)
  • Focus on English: Strong English skills are essential as it's the medium of instruction
  • Develop Study Skills: Learn effective note-taking, time management, and exam strategies
  • Join Study Groups: Collaborate with local students to learn from their approaches
  • Understand Assessment Methods: Familiarize yourself with Singapore's examination formats and requirements
  • Cultural Adaptation: Understand the educational culture, which values diligence and academic excellence

Our specialized tutoring programs are designed to ease this transition, identifying gaps in knowledge and building the necessary skills to excel in Singapore's system.

What health insurance do international students need?

International students in Singapore should have comprehensive health insurance coverage:

  • Many schools and institutions provide basic insurance plans as part of the tuition fees
  • However, these basic plans may have limitations on coverage amounts and types of treatment
  • We recommend obtaining additional private insurance that covers:
    • Hospitalization and surgical expenses
    • Outpatient treatment
    • Emergency evacuation
    • Personal accident coverage
    • Travel insurance for visits home
  • Several local and international insurers offer student-specific health plans in Singapore
  • Typical costs range from SGD 300-800 per year depending on coverage

Singapore has an excellent healthcare system but medical costs can be high for non-residents, making insurance essential.

Can international students work while studying in Singapore?

The rules for international students working in Singapore vary depending on the type of educational institution:

  • University/Polytechnic Students: May work part-time up to 16 hours per week during term and full-time during vacations without additional work permits
  • Private Education Institution Students: Must obtain a Work Pass Exemption from the Ministry of Manpower (MOM) to work part-time, and only if their institution is on the approved list
  • Primary/Secondary/JC Students: Generally not permitted to work

Important restrictions:

  • Working at bars, nightclubs, massage parlors, or gambling establishments is not permitted
  • Working beyond permitted hours can result in revocation of the Student's Pass
  • Studies should remain the primary focus

We recommend focusing on academics, especially during the initial adjustment period, before considering part-time work.

What cultural adjustments should international students prepare for?

Singapore has a unique culture that blends Chinese, Malay, Indian, and Western influences. Here are some cultural aspects international students should be aware of:

  • Academic Environment: Highly competitive and achievement-oriented with great respect for teachers
  • Multilingual Society: While English is widely spoken, you'll hear Mandarin, Malay, Tamil, and Singlish (local English creole)
  • Rules and Regulations: Singapore is known for strict enforcement of rules and cleanliness
  • Food Culture: Diverse cuisine with communal dining being common (hawker centers are cultural institutions)
  • Weather: Hot and humid year-round (prepare for regular rainfall and indoor air conditioning)
  • Public Behavior: Politeness and reservation are valued; public displays of emotion may be seen as inappropriate
  • Punctuality: Being on time is important in both academic and social settings

Tips for adjustment:

  • Participate in orientation programs offered by your school
  • Join student clubs or activities to make local friends
  • Explore different parts of Singapore to understand the culture
  • Be open to trying new foods and experiences
  • Respect local customs and traditions
How can parents stay involved in their child's education while abroad?

For parents whose children are studying in Singapore while they remain in their home country, staying involved in their education is important:

  • School Communication Systems: Most Singapore schools have online portals where parents can track grades, attendance, and homework
  • Regular Video Calls: Schedule consistent times to check in on academic progress and wellbeing
  • Parent-Teacher Conferences: Participate remotely in parent-teacher meetings
  • Local Guardian: Appoint a trusted local guardian who can attend school events and monitor your child's progress
  • Tuition Progress Reports: Work with tutoring services like ours that provide detailed progress reports and feedback
  • Holiday Planning: Plan visits during school holidays to meet with teachers in person
  • Stay Informed: Familiarize yourself with the Singapore curriculum and assessment methods

Our services include regular updates for parents, and we're happy to arrange video conferences to discuss your child's academic progress and areas for improvement.
